The core idea

Adaptive Friction

Sentinel scores every transaction against each customer's own financial fingerprint — not a generic rulebook. Friction only appears when behavior genuinely deviates.

Routine behavior

Same device, familiar recipient, typical amount and hour. The transfer completes instantly — no popups, no PIN re-entry, no OTP. Security stays invisible.

Sentinel scores every transfer

Anomalous behavior

Large amount, unfamiliar recipient, odd hour, or right after a security change. Sentinel holds the transfer and explains — in plain language — exactly why.

End to end

What happens when a transfer gets held

Step 1

Transaction hits the scoring engine

Every transfer is scored in real time against the customer's own behavioral fingerprint, not a fixed threshold shared across all users.

Step 2

An anomaly returns a "held" status

An LLM turns the raw risk signals into one honest, plain-language sentence — the same explanation a careful human would give.

Step 3

The customer decides

"Yes, it's me" triggers a quick identity check and releases the funds. "No, block this" instantly freezes money movement — no funds ever leave the account.

Step 4

Sentinel explains itself, always

Whatever the outcome, the customer can open the Trust Panel and see exactly what data drove the decision — and remove it if they choose.
